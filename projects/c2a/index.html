üéØ The Challenge

Meta-RL agents struggle in high-dimensional environments because they treat all information equally. In reality, only a small subset of dimensions causally influence rewards. For example, in autonomous driving with 45 observable vehicles, typically only 2-3 actually affect lane-changing decisions.

üî¨ Technical Approach

Core Innovation

C2A discovers causal structure through VarLiNGAM's temporal model:

$$x_t = \sum_{\tau=0}^{p} B_\tau x_{t-\tau} + e_t$$

where \(B_0\) captures instantaneous effects and \(B_\tau\) captures time-lagged dependencies.

The causal importance \(c = |b^{(r)}| / ||b^{(r)}||_1\) guides exploration through ADCR:

$$H_{ADCR} = \sum_{i=1}^{d_a} w_i(z,c) \cdot H(a_i|s,z)$$

üîç Why C2A Works

üéØ Focused Exploration

By identifying causal dimensions, C2A allocates 80% of exploration budget to the 10-20% of dimensions that matter.

üîó Bidirectional Integration

VarLiNGAM and CausalVAE guide each other, creating a unified understanding of temporal and instantaneous causality.

üìà Sample Efficiency

Theoretical guarantee: O(d_c log d_c) samples vs O(d log d) for standard methods, where d_c << d.

üé® Interpretability

Discovered causal structures provide insights into task dynamics, enabling better debugging and transfer.
